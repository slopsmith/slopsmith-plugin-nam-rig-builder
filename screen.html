            Global FX layered around every tone of every song. Pre
            runs first (sees the raw DI — typical place for input gate / compressor / tuner).
            Post runs after the cab IR (typical place for limiter / room
            reverb / headphone EQ). Each piece supports the same Library / VST / Bypass / reorder
            controls as a song chain. Signal flow:
            guitar → master_pre → song → master_post → output.
        

        

            How to use:
            (1) ＋ Add piece → pick a Rocksmith effect OR a VST plug-in.
            VST pieces are ready to use immediately —
            just hit 🎛 Edit VST to tweak its parameters.
            Rocksmith pieces start unassigned: click Assign…
            then pick a NAM/IR from your library or paste a VST plug-in path.
            (2) Master pieces are global — they don't appear on the Pending or Gear catalog tabs.
        

        

            🎸 AMP must be on for master to apply. If you flip the
            AMP button mid-song, Rig Builder now auto-reloads the
            chain (including master pre/post) ~1 second later — no need to leave and re-enter. Disable with
            window.__rbAmpAutoApply = false if it ever misfires.

VST / Audio Unit plugins

            

                Scan your installed VST3 / Audio Unit plugins once here — the
                results feed the 📚 Library picker (Plugins tab) on every gear.
                Scanning instantiates each plugin to validate it, so it can take
                up to a minute; a broken plugin may make it time out (check the
                console for the offending path).
            

            

                
                
            

        


        

            
Auto-mode policy

            
            
        


        

            

                Chain preloader EXPERIMENTAL
            

            

                Pre-loads every tone of the song into one big chain at song start,
                then switches between tones by toggling bypass instead of reloading
                NAMs. Eliminates the tone-change spike
                completely (no mute/fade workaround needed) at the cost of
                higher steady-state memory and CPU — every NAM stays loaded.
                Recommended only on M1/M2/M3 with feather
                or nano models.
            

            

                ⚠️ Requires AMP off on the song player — Rig Builder
                drives the engine directly in this mode. If it misbehaves, turn it off
                here and you're back to the cooperative path. Kill-switch in DevTools:
                window.__rbMegaChain = false.
            

            
        


        

            
NAM model size (CPU vs realism)

            

                Picks which variant tone3000 downloads serve when multiple sizes
                exist for the same capture. Smaller sizes load and render
                much faster — relevant
                because every mid-song tone change reloads the whole chain. If
                you see audio spikes or lag on tone changes, switch to
                feather and
                re-run Batch all (mode: all) on the Dashboard so the
                existing songs grab the lighter models.
            

            
            

                Existing downloaded models are not changed. Only newly downloaded
                captures (auto-download or batch) honour the new setting.
            

        


        

            
Cab makeup gain

            

                Cab simulations (IRs) naturally attenuate the amp signal by 10-15 dB —
                that's the cab absorbing energy + rolling off highs, same as a real cab.
                This slider boosts the IR stage's output to compensate. +3 dB
                is conservative; +6 dB brings it close to
                the bypass level.
            

            

                ⚠️ The boost lives on the IR stage, so bypassing the cab also boosts the
                signal by the same amount (the passthrough sees the gain too). That's why
                the slider caps at +9 dB — a sudden bypass with the slider at max would jump 9 dB.
                Keep it ≤ +6 dB if you toggle cab bypass often.
            

            
            

                Applies to every IR / Rocksmith-IR stage in your chains. Takes effect on the
                next song-load / Listen-tap (chain JSON is rebuilt with the new gain).
